## Beyond the Bow: Samuel’s Modern Insight

In Spain, a greeting is practically a full-contact sport—you’re diving directly into a hug or the classic *dos besos* (two kisses) before you’ve even learned the person’s name. It’s warm, it’s vibrant, and it’s an immediate, unapologetic invasion of your personal bubble. 

Contrast this with the Japanese *Ojigi* (bow). It is a masterclass in "Respectful Distance." You acknowledge the other person’s presence, honor their space, and maintain a perimeter of elegant neutrality without ever crossing the physical threshold. 

Honestly, in our hyper-connected, post-pandemic digital world where everyone is perpetually in each other's faces online, bowing is the ultimate "Zero-Contact" greeting. It’s the physical equivalent of acknowledging an email without having to jump on a Zoom call—perfectly polite, deeply respectful, and entirely contact-free.
